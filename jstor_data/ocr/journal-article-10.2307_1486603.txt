<plain_text><page sequence="1">UPDATED BIBLIOGRAPHY OF ALEXANDER ALTMANN' S PUBLISHED WRITINGS Alexander Altmann passed away six years ago, yet his presence is still very much felt by all who work in the field of medieval and early modern Jewish philosophy and mysticism. The recent publication of his early essays on Jewish theology and the crisis of German Jewry in the thirties will bring him to the attention of a still wider circle of readers. His place among the foremost scholars of Judaic studies in our century is assured. Many members of the Association for Jewish Studies were fortunate to have known Professor Altmann personally. He took an active role in the meetings of the Association, both as a speaker and as a faithful attender of the sessions. He contributed greatly to the level of discussion at these meetings, helping to turn them into learning sessions for all present. In one of his last conversations, he spoke about the talk he planned to give at the 1987 conference. It is thus fitting that the AJS Review publish a completed update of Dr. Altmann's bibliography. It was prepared by his devoted brother Dr. Manfred Altman, Chairman of the Board of Governors of the Institute of Jewish Studies in University College, London.
